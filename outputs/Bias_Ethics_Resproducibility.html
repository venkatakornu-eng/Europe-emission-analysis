0.0.1 What this file is for

This week is hands-on with Bias,Fairness,Ethics & Resproducibility. We have Did:

  • Detect imbalance vs. bias and relate it to project purpose.
  • Explain fairness notions (representation vs outcome fairness).
  • Perform hypothesis tests and interpret confidence intervals.
  • Use a fixed random seed to make analyses reproducible.
  • Observe how different seeds affect results and train/test splits.
  • Save a lightweight reproducibility log (session info) to your repo.

2.1 Conclusion

  • Changing the random seed changes which subset of data gets sampled, slightly altering the model. Fixing a seed (e.g., set.seed(42)) makes results reproducible.
  • Equal data distribution across countries supports fair analysis.
  • Simple linear regression indicates minor year-to-year variation in emissions
