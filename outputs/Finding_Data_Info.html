2.1 Understanding Each Criterion

  • Origin & Authority: Identify who created or published the dataset, and why it exists. Check for official sources or credible research institutions.
  • Coverage & Completeness: Consider whether the data spans a sufficient period, geography, and number of observations for your analysis. Missing years or regions may signal gaps.
  • Timeliness & Stability: When was it last updated? Has the structure changed over time? Outdated or unstable sources may reduce reliability.
  • Format & Access: Ensure the format (CSV, JSON, etc.) can be opened reproducibly with R/Python. Record any URLs or APIs used.
  • Documentation & Versioning: Look for accompanying documentation (README, codebook, changelog) or version numbers that clarify structure.
  • License & Attribution: Record the license name (e.g., CC BY 4.0, OGL v3.0) and link. Copy the required attribution statement if available.
  • Sensitivity & Ethics: Note whether the data includes personal, identifiable, or ethically sensitive information. If yes, explain how you’ll handle it responsibly.
